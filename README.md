# Chevron Actuator Frequency Optimization

## Overview
This project investigates the frequency response of MEMS chevron (thermal) actuators used in optical switching applications. The goal is to improve high-speed actuation by analyzing thermal and mechanical limits and enhancing heat dissipation using fin structures.

## Key Contributions
- Analysis of input waveform effects on thermal actuation efficiency  
- Decoupled thermal and mechanical modeling of chevron actuators  
- Combined thermo-mechanical frequency response characterization  
- Design map identifying thermal vs mechanical limitation regimes  
- Fin-based thermal optimization for improved bandwidth  

## Results
- Square-wave inputs provide higher efficiency at high frequencies  
- Thermal dynamics dominate and limit actuator speed  
- Mechanical resonance is typically inaccessible in standard designs  
- Fin structures improve heat dissipation and thermal bandwidth  

## Repository Structure
- `models/` – Thermal and mechanical models  
- `simulations/` – Frequency response and efficiency scripts  
- `plots/` – Generated figures and design maps  
- `report/` – LaTeX paper and results
